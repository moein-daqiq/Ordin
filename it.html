What is IT operations for a company?

IT operations is how a business manages the tools employees need to work — laptops, accounts, permissions, security, and the process of giving and removing access. For most companies, the biggest pain is not “technology” — it’s the workflow and control.

Core parts of IT for teams

Device management

Track laptops, phones, and equipment: who has what, when it was issued, and when it’s returned.

Account provisioning

Create access for tools like email, Slack, Microsoft 365, Google Workspace, and internal systems — with approvals.

Permissions & access control

Ensure employees only have the access they need — reducing risk and keeping control as the team grows.

Onboarding workflows

New joiner steps: request device, set up accounts, confirm access, and record completion.

Offboarding workflows

Remove access on time, recover devices, and document completion — critical for security and compliance.

Security & audit trail

Record who approved access, when accounts were created or removed, and what devices were issued.

Why IT is important (especially in the UK)

Poor IT processes create security risks and business disruption. In the UK, data handling and access control also matter for compliance and customer trust — especially when staff change frequently.

What happens when IT is not managed properly?

Security risk

Former employees keep access to email or systems, leading to data leakage and serious reputational damage.

Slow onboarding

New hires waste days without a laptop or accounts, reducing productivity and creating a bad first impression.

Lost devices & costs

Without tracking, equipment goes missing and replacement costs rise quickly as the team grows.
