What is HR (and what is an HRIS)?

HR is how a business manages its people — from onboarding and documentation to policies, leave, performance, and offboarding. An HRIS (Human Resources Information System) is the platform that stores and manages those HR processes and records in one place.

In the UK, HR matters because employment rules are strict, documentation is critical, and problems often become legal issues if records are missing or inconsistent.

Core parts of HR in the UK

People records

A single source of truth for each employee: role, salary, start date, right-to-work notes, emergency contacts, and key documents.

Contracts & changes

Offer letters, employment contracts, amendments, promotions, salary changes — all tracked with approvals and history.

Onboarding & offboarding

Tasks, document collection, checklists, equipment requests, and clean handovers — so nothing is missed.

Policies & acknowledgements

Policies, handbooks, and required acknowledgements tracked per employee — critical in UK disputes.

Leave & absence

Holiday requests, approvals, sickness tracking, and leave balances — aligned with UK statutory entitlements.

Approvals & audit trail

Every HR action needs traceability: who approved what, when, and why — especially for payroll and compliance.

Why HR is important for UK employers

UK HR is not only about “people management” — it’s about protecting the business with the right records and fair processes. When HR is organised, payroll runs smoother, disputes reduce, and hiring becomes repeatable.

What happens when HR is weak?

Legal & tribunal risk

Missing contracts, unclear policies, or inconsistent treatment can become employment tribunal cases.

Operational chaos

People data scattered across emails and spreadsheets causes delays, errors, and confusion across teams.

Payroll & compliance mistakes

Wrong employee details, missing approvals, and poor leave tracking lead to incorrect payroll and disputes.
