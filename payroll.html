What is payroll?

Payroll is the process of calculating pay accurately, applying the right deductions, producing payslips, paying workers on time, and keeping clear records. It’s not just “sending salary” — it’s the system that protects employees and keeps the business compliant.

Core parts of UK payroll

People & contract data

Pay terms, start dates, roles, and worker details — the foundation for every payroll run.

Payroll inputs

Hours, overtime, bonuses, expenses, and leave — captured and approved before processing.

Gross pay calculation

Accurate pay calculations based on contract terms and approved inputs.

Deductions & contributions

Taxes and deductions applied correctly with the right records and approvals.

Payslips & payments

Payslip-ready output and on-time payouts — clarity for workers and finance teams.

Reporting & audit trail

Clear records of what changed, who approved it, and when — ready for audits and disputes.

Why payroll matters

Payroll is a trust system. When it runs smoothly, teams stay confident and operations scale. When it’s weak, businesses face disputes, rework, and compliance risk.

What happens if payroll runs late or incorrectly?

Late pay
Loss of trust, staff frustration, potential legal issues, and reputational damage.
Wrong pay
Underpayments/overpayments, disputes, corrections, and time wasted fixing preventable errors.
Missing approvals
Changes happen informally, making payroll inconsistent and hard to defend later.
Weak records
When issues arise, evidence is scattered — difficult to prove what happened and why.

How ORDIN supports payroll

ORDIN connects payroll inputs with contracts, HR records, and approvals — so pay runs are cleaner, changes are traceable, and every key action has an audit trail.
